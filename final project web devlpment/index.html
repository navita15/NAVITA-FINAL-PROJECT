About Us

Samsung was founded by Lee Byung-chul in 1938 as a trading company. Over the next three decades, the group diversified into areas including food processing, textiles, insurance, securities, and retail. Samsung entered the electronics industry in the late 1960s and the construction and shipbuilding industries in the mid-1970s; these areas would drive its subsequent growth

Our Services

REFERIGERATOR

Samsung has revolutionized the realm of refrigeration with its avant-garde lineup of appliances that go beyond mere food preservation. By seamlessly integrating technology and convenience, Samsung refrigerators have become essential components of modern kitchens. Featuring state-of-the-art features like FlexZone™ compartments, Family Hub™ touchscreen displays, and Twin Cooling Plus™ systems, these refrigerators offer optimal temperature control, customizability, and connectivity.

TELEVISION

In the world of home entertainment, Samsung's television offerings have consistently set new standards for picture quality, design, and interactivity. Their QLED and Neo QLED TVs showcase groundbreaking Quantum Dot technology, ushering in an era of vibrant and true-to-life colors.

MOBILES

Samsung's prowess in mobile technology is exemplified by its diverse range of smartphones that cater to various user preferences and needs. The Galaxy series, renowned for its cutting-edge features and sleek designs, consistently pushes the boundaries of what a mobile device can achieve.

LAPTOPS

Samsung's line of laptops embodies a perfect fusion of style and substance, catering to both casual users and professionals seeking powerful computing solutions. The Galaxy Book series, for instance, boasts a harmonious blend of sleek aesthetics and formidable performance.

Our Vision

Samsung is committed to complying with local laws and regulations as well as applying a strict global code of conduct to all employees. It believes that ethical management is not only a tool for responding to the rapid changes in the global business environment, but also a vehicle for building trust with its various stakeholders including customers, shareholders, employees, business partners and local communities. With an aim to become one of the most ethical companies In the world, Samsung continues to train its employees and operate monitoring systems, while practicing fair and transparent corporate management.

Our Team

Lee Byung-chul

CEO and Co-owner

Lee Byung-chul was born on 12 February 1910 in Uiryeong County, South Gyeongsang Province, Korean Empire. He was born the youngest son of four siblings to father Lee Chan-woo and mother Kwon Jae-lim. He was the son of a wealthy land-owning yangban family (a branch of the Gyeongju Lee clan). He attended highschool at Joongdong High School in Seoul, and then college at Waseda University in Tokyo but did not complete his degree

The first step of the Federation of Korean Industries was established in August 1961. The association was founded by Samsung Group chairman Lee Byung-chul. Later in life, Byung-chul served as chairman of the Federation of Korean Industries and was known as the richest man in Korea.

Lee Kun-hee

CEO and Co-owner

Lee Kun-hee (Korean: 이건희; Hanja: 李健熙, Korean: [iːɡʌnɣi]; 9 January 1942 – 25 October 2020) was a South Korean business magnate who served as the chairman of Samsung Group from 1987 to 2008, and again from 2010 until his death in 2020. He is also credited with the transformation of Samsung to one of the world's largest business entities that engages in semiconductors, smartphones, electronics, shipbuilding, construction, and other businesses.

Lee was convicted for having paid bribes to president Roh Tae-woo in 1996. He was subsequently pardoned by president Kim Young-sam.[5] On 14 January 2008, Korean police raided Lee's home and office in an ongoing probe into accusations that Samsung was responsible for a slush fund used to bribe influential prosecutors, judges, and political figures in South Korea.On 4 April 2008, Lee denied allegations against him in the scandal.
